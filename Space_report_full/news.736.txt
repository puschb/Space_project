International Space Station
---------------------------

Expedition 50 continues. Soyuz MS-02 is docked at Poisk. Soyuz MS-03 is docked at Rassvet.
Progress MS-05 is docked at Pirs. Dragon CRS-10 was berthed at Harmony nadir until Mar 18.

The NanoRacks NRCSD-10 deployer was extracted from the Kibo airlock on Mar 6.
Four SpireGlobal Lemur satellites and the TechEdSat-5 satellite were deployed
from it the same day. The satellites were launched aboard HTV-6 in December.
TechEdSat-5 carries an ExoBrake experiment to perform a semi-controlled reentry.

The Dextre and Canadarm-2 robot arms were used to unload the experiments in the
Dragon external cargo trunk on Feb 25-Mar 7 in a rather complicated sequence
involving Dextre's two main arms and its EOTP (Enhanced ORU Temp Platform, a
storage position). The experiments were installed on the Express Logistics Carriers
ELC-1 and ELC-4 on the station truss. Older experiments from ELC-1, ELC-2
and ELC-4 were removed for disposal.

The OPALS laser comm payload was launched in 2014; the RRM Robotic
Refuelling Mission experiment in 2011. The MISSE FSE is an adapter that
mounted the MISSE 7 and 8 materials exposure experiments; it was
launched with ELC-2 in 2009. These packages, with a total mass of 811
kg, were destroyed on reentry when the Dragon trunk was jettisoned
following Dragon's deorbit burn.

Dragon was unberthed from the Harmony module at 2145 UTC Mar 18
and released by Canadarm-2 at 0911 UTC Mar 19. After a deorbit burn
at 1355 UTC it splashed down in the Pacific near 31 44N 121 15W
at 1446 UTC, carrying 1652 kg of ISS return cargo. This return
cargo includes 104 kg of unspecified EVA equipment, per Stephen
Clark of spaceflightnow.com.


NROL-79
-------

ULA launched Atlas V serial AV-068 on Mar 1 from Vandenberg, carrying a
National Reconnaissance Office payload to orbit on NRO launch NROL-79.
The payload is thought to be an NRO/Navy signals intelligence system
code-named INTRUDER consisting of a pair of satellites in 1010 x 1200 km
x 63 deg orbit. Both satellites were observed by hobbyists on Mar 3. The
Centaur second stage was deorbited south of Australia on the first orbit
with impact around 1956 UTC.

KT-2
----

On Mar 2 China's CASIC missile agency launched the KT-2, a new orbital
launch vehicle developed by CASIC's 4th Academy. 
It placed a small satellite, Tiankun-1, in orbit.
Tiankun 1, built by the CASIC 2nd Academy (CCMETA), is a test
of a new satellite bus and probably has a mass of a few hundred kg.

So far as I know the only large missile developed previously by CASIC
was the DF-21 and its JL-1 submarine sibling; the KT-1 and KZ-1 launch
vehicles are thought to be based on the DF-21. It has been suggested
that KT-2 is based on the DF-31 missile which was originally developed
by CASIC's rival CALT which built the CZ-11 rocket based on that
missile. This is a bit strange, but would be similar to the way China's
leaders made  Beijing's CALT and the Shanghai bureau develop the
rival-but-similar CZ-2 and FB-1 launch vehicles in the 1970s.


PSLV launch
-----------

Identification of the 104 satellites from the PSLV launch is continuing
with most of the Flock-3p now associated with a catalog number. The
Nayif-1 satellite with its Funcube-5 amateur radio payload has been
designated Emirates-OSCAR-88 by AMSAT. The SpireGlobal Lemur satellites
are not yet identified.

Sentinel-2B
-----------

The ESA Sentinel-2B imaging satellite, part of the EU's Copernicus Earth observing
system, was launched by Vega VV09 on Mar 7.

Echostar 23
-----------

The Echostar 23 communications sat will provide television services to Brazil and Latin
America for Echostar Corp. It was launched by Falcon 9 F31; the first stage on this
mission did not have landing legs and no recovery was attempted.

IGS R5
-------

At 0120 UTC Mar 17, Japan launched the Joho shushu eisei Reda 5-goki (IGS Radar 5) spy
satellite into polar orbit.


WGS 9
-----

Wideband Global Satcom 9 is to be added to the US DoD communications
satellite constellation. This WGS was partly funded by allied countries,
although it is still owned and operated by USAF. A United Launch
Alliance Delta 4 sent the satellite aloft on Mar 19, first into a 185 x
6097 km x 27.6 deg parking orbit and then to a 430 x 44262 km x 27.0 deg
supersynchronous transfer orbit. The Delta 377 second stage was
deorbited over the Pacific near the Phillipines with reentry around 1230
UTC.

Cassini
-------

Cassini continues ring close approaches in a 86600 x 1226000 km x 63.6
deg orbit around Saturn. This 'Molniya'-style frozen orbit exploits a
peculiarity of orbital mechanics: an object in orbit with inclination i
around an oblate (flattened) planet like  Earth or Saturn causes the
orbital ellipse to rotate in its orbital plane at a rate proportional to
4 - 5 sin-squared i, which is zero if i = 63.4 degrees. Using this
'magic' inclination keeps the close approach to Saturn in the ring plane
instead of letting it drift to higher latitudes.  On Apr 22 Cassini will
make its last close flyby of Titan; this will lower the orbital
periapsis to only 2600 km above Saturn's cloud tops and begin the
Proximal Orbits phase of the mission. It will also mark a record
close approach to Saturn, finally beating the record it set in Jun 2004
when it came within 18100 km during Saturn arrival.
